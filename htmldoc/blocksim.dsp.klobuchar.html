blocksim.dsp.klobuchar module

blocksim.dsp.klobuchar.klobuchar(phi, lbd, elev, azimuth, tow, alpha, beta) → float[source]

Function for computing an Ionospheric range correction for the GPS L1 frequency from the parameters broadcasted in the GPS Navigation Message.

References: Klobuchar, J.A., (1996) « Ionosphercic Effects on GPS », in

Parkinson, Spilker (ed), « Global Positioning System Theory and Applications, pp.513-514.

ICD-GPS-200, Rev. C, (1997), pp. 125-128 NATO, (1991), « Technical Characteristics of the NAVSTAR GPS »,

pp. A-6-31 - A-6-33

Paramètres

  • phi (rad) – Geodetic latitude of receiver

  • lbd (rad) – Geodetic longitude of receiver

  • elev (rad) – Elevation angle of satellite

  • azimuth (rad) – Geodetic azimuth of satellite

  • tow (s) – Time of Week

  • alpha[3] – The coefficients of a cubic equation representing the amplitude of the vertical delay (4 coefficients)

  • beta[3] – The coefficients of a cubic equation representing the period of the model (4 coefficients)

Renvoie

Ionospheric delay for the L1 frequency (sec)

Exemples

>>> alpha = np.array([0.3820e-7, 0.1490e-7, -0.1790e-6, 0.0000])
>>> beta = np.array([0.1430e6, 0.0000, -0.3280e6, 0.1130e6])
>>> elev = 20*pi/180
>>> azimuth = 210*pi/180
>>> phi = 40*pi/180
>>> lbd = 260*pi/180
>>> tow = 0.0
>>> dIon1 = klobuchar(phi, lbd, elev, azimuth, tow, alpha, beta)
>>> dIon1
6.93560658...e-08
